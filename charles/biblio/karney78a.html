Abstract
We consider the equation governing the mode structure of Alfvén waves. Since the Alfvén speed is a function of density, the compressional Alfvén wave changes from evanescent to propagating at some point in the plasma. However the presence of the shear Alfvén resonance changes this cutoff into a cutoff-resonance-cutoff triplet. By analytically and numerically studying the properties of the governing equation, we determine the absorption due to the resonance and Q for the eigenmode. We show that heating is possible using relatively high-Q eigenmodes.
