Stochastic Motion of Particles in Tandem Mirror Devices

Reprint: physd83a

BibTeX Entry
Abstract
Stochastic motion of collisionless guiding centers in tandem mirror devices is analyzed in terms of a nonlinear mapping of particle positions on the equatorial plane. Local stability analysis determines the appearance of various patterns of particle trajectories. Numerical observation of radial motion of particles makes it possible to estimate the rate of stochastic diffusion.
