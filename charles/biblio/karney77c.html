Stochastic Ion Heating by a Perpendicularly Propagating Electrostatic Wave

Abstract
The motion of an ion in the presence of a constant magnetic field and a perpendicularly propagating electrostatic wave with frequency several times the ion cyclotron frequency is shown to become stochastic for fields satisfying E/B0 > ¼(Ω/ω)1/3(ω/k). This stochasticity condition is independent of how close ω is to a cyclotron harmonic. Applications of current interest in supplementary heating of plasmas with rf power near the lower-hybrid frequency are suggested.

An earlier version of this work was published with the same title as M.I.T. Rept. RLE PRR 76/24 (July 1976).
