Workshops and Other Activities

Workshop on Sharing & Showing at Hort Shows -- Friday, September 16, 2016, 9:30 am - 12 noon
Debbie Jordan and Kathy Enquist will lead the workshop covering all aspects of exhibiting in horticulture shows including choosing, identifying, conditioning and entering your plant material. Show entry card completion will be reviewed. Entry cards and green Perrier bottles will be available for use in the September Hort Show. Required for Provisionals and attendance is expected of their proposers and all members of the Horticulture Committee. All club members are invited. RSVP to Louisa Clayton.


Bulb Forcing Workshop -- Friday, October 21, 2016, 10:30 am
Our in-club bulb forcing expert(s) will guide us through potting bulbs for forcing for the Philadelphia Flower Show or for your own enjoyment, and the preparation of entry cards for the show. This workshop is required for all provisional members and provided at no cost to them. Provisional proposers are expected to attend. We encourage all our members to participate. This year's bulbs are Crocus Blue Pearl, Crocus Gipsy Girl and Tulip Foxtrot. The cost is $20 per pot. Let's continue to elevate our club's presence at the Show! RSVP Kathy Enquist.


Horticulture Committee Terrarium Workshop -- Friday, November 4, 2016, 10:00 a.m.
PHS Meadowbrook Farm, Jenkintown, PA
We will travel in car pools to PHS Meadowbrook Farm for a workshop to create our own terrariums for exhibiting at the Philadelphia Flower Show in March, at the May in-club Hort Show, and for our own enjoyment. Cost: approximately $35-$45 per person depending on your choice of plants and container. You may provide your own glass container instead of purchasing one on site. Container size not to exceed 10 inches in diameter/diagonal inside measurement. We will lunch together at a nearby restaurant following the workshop. RSVP to Louisa Clayton by November 1, 2016.


Philadelphia Flower Show Entry Preparation -- Friday, February 3, 2017 10:30 - Noon
The countdown to entering the Philadelphia Flower Show begins with a discussion of the steps to bring bulbs into bloom, grooming and transporting tips. This workshop is open to the entire club and all who potted bulbs at the October 21, 2016 workshop are required to attend. The Philadelphia Flower Show is only five weeks away! RSVP Penny Thomas.


Lunch and Learn at The Present Day Club -- Tuesday, February 28, 2017 11:00 am
Andrea Filippone will speak to us about boxwoods and their care, insect and disease problems, best varieties to plant, and ideas for incorporating them into your landscape design. Andrea is President of AJF Design an all encompassing design firm located in Pottersville, NJ that blends Landscape Architecture, Interior Design and Architecture. She is also a director of the American Boxwood Society and propagates boxwood for her nursery's global collection. Her garden, Jardin De Buis, has been featured in several books and magazines. Please feel free to bring guests to this very special event. Cost: $40 per person including lunch. RSVP to Jane Murphy with check by February 23, 2017.


Horticulture Committee Planning Luncheon -- Friday, March 24, 2017 11:00 am
The Horticulture Committee will gather over lunch to discuss plans and activities for 2017-2018 when our species of the year is Peonies. RSVP


Horticulture Committee Passing Workshop -- Monday, April 10, 2017 10:00 am
We will review the fine points of the art of passing in preparation for our April and May Shows including review of the American Daffodil Society's Classification System. Anyone scheduled to pass at the April or May Horticulture Show must attend. The entire club is encouraged to attend, especially members of the Horticulture Committee to prepare for being passers in the future.


Softwood Propagation Workshop -- June 2017 (date TBD) 10:00 am
The Horticulture Committee will host a softwood propagation workshop at Bowman's Hill Wildflower Preserve lead by Bob Mahler, Nursery Manager, to start plants in preparation for the GCA Zone IV Flower Show plant exchange in 2018. Date and details TBD. RSVP Louisa Clayton.

One stem or branch per exhibit, unless otherwise noted, of a plant grown primarily for foliage. All flowers and buds to be removed.
1. Annuals
2. Perennials
3. Boxwood (Buxus)
4. A collection of three foliage stems or branches. These can be woody, herbaceous, annual, or geophyte with one required to be Boxwood. Class D: Collection featuring a New Jersey Native Aster (new botanical name is Symphyotrichum)

Collection of 3 stems or branches featuring one flowering stem of a New Jersey Native Aster or cultivar of a New Jersey Native Aster, and two stems of two different plants (not Aster), which may or may not be in flower. Cut specimen collection to be displayed in a clear glass container supplied by the exhibitor. Key card required. Judging will be based on 60% horticultural and 40% artistic merit.

Class E: Roses (Rosa)
1. Single stems. One bloom on one five-leafed stem, grown without pesticides.
2. Single stems. One bloom on one five-leafed stem, grown with pesticides.
3. Single stem. More than one bloom on a five-leafed stem, grown without pesticides.
4. Single stem. More than one bloom on a five-leafed stem, grown with pesticides.

Class F: Succulent Container Garden
A collection of at least 3 different succulents displayed in a trough* not to exceed 12" in diameter. Key card required. Judging will be based on 60% horticultural and 40% artistic merit.
*Troughs shall be defined as a hyper tufa, stone-like or cement container of any shape.


Class G: A Bloom from the Garden of a Provisional
A single flowering stem from the garden of a provisional, entered by that provisional with the assistance of her proposer.

Class H: Family Collection from My Garden
One stem (flowering or not) of 5 different plant families* from your garden. Key card required. Tube vases to be provided. Limited to 3 entries. Preregistration to Kathy Enquist required.

*Some examples of plant families: Asteraceae (Daisy family), Geraniaceae (Geranium family), Malvaceae (Mallow family), Polemoniaceae (Phlox family), Rosaceae (Rose family).
